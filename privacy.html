Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. What we collect

Openclaw accesses YouTube channel data (channel ID, video metadata, upload status) solely to upload AI-generated videos on behalf of the authenticated channel owner. No personal data is stored on external servers.

2. How we use Google user data

Openclaw uses the YouTube Data API v3 under the OAuth 2.0 protocol. Access is limited to channels explicitly authorised by the user. We use this access only to upload videos and read channel analytics. We do not sell, share, or transfer any data to third parties.

3. Data storage

OAuth tokens are stored locally on the user's own machine. No data is transmitted to or stored on any third-party service beyond Google's own APIs.

4. Data deletion

You can revoke access at any time via Google Account Permissions. Locally stored tokens can be deleted by removing the token files from your installation.
